On this page, you will find samples of my print design work from when I was a newspaper page designer and from a magazine design course I took at the University of Missouri. Due to the nature of the newspaper deadlines and the large volume of pages produced on a nightly basis, I often did not have the time to save work samples before they were deleted from the system. While I only have a few newspaper samples, I regularly designed news, feature, sports, and special sections, including covers.

Magazine Design

Single Page Redesign (View PDF )

One of my earliest magazine assignments was to redesign a single page from any magazine of my choice. The original page is on the left, while my redesign is on the right. My choices allowed for more space than what was available on the original page so I added some extra content. While there are things I like about my redesign, there are things I would change. First, I would not be not have the page split between white and red backgrounds. Instead, I would use the red to offset one of the sidebar sections and then move the other to break-up the dialogue. Second, I would rework the folio to take up less space. Honestly, the original layout works well, and I would probably have done something similar.

Magazine Photostory Design (View PDF )

For this assignment, I had to design a four-spread (eight-page) photostory. Overall, I'm still fairly happy with my design. The story and photos were provided by my instructor, but everything else is "from scratch." The orange and green colors used throughout were taken from the photo on the first spread. If I were doing this assignment today, I would probably do the photos a little differently so that there was more consistency throughout for both the photo sizes and the columns. You can view the full design by following the PDF link above.

Concept Design (View PDF )

While the story was provided for this assignment, we had to come up with the artwork — as a team. Our group illustration, which can be seen on the left page, isn't cohesive. While we agreed on the concept for our main illustration, we weren't clear about how to create the illustrations so the styles would all match. Graphic design is not a strength for me; however, I managed to create the laptop in the main image and the gadgets used in the rest of my design. The most fun I had with this assignment was experimenting with the typefaces used and adding text to the illustrations. It's not my favorite design, but it represents a valuable lesson about designing in teams. You can follow the PDF link above to see the full design.

Magazine Redesign (View PDF )

My final assignment was to redesign the cover, index page, a department spread, and feature page of a magazine. I'm still happy with the cover and index pages, but there are some things I would do differently on the department and feature pages. On the concept page, I would rework a few of the elements so that it all flowed together better. On the feature page, I would redesign the islands. Every element on that page is type, including the arrow going across the top of the page. Were I to design this today, the paths to the islands would be replaced with arrows similar to those across the top of the page. For the islands, I would stick to the word cloud idea used for the states. Follow the PDF link above to see all of the redesign.
