Print Design Descriptions

Single Page Redesign

One of my earliest magazine assignments was to redesign a single magazine page. The original page is on the left, and my redesign is on the right. My choices allowed for more space than what was available on the original page so I added extra content. While there are things I like about my redesign, there are things I would change.

First, I would not be not have the page split between white and red backgrounds. Instead, I would use the red to offset one of the sidebar sections and then move the other to break-up the dialogue. Second, I would rework the folio to take up less space. Honestly, the original layout works well, and I would probably do something similar.

Magazine Photostory Design

For this assignment, I had to design a four-spread (eight-page) photostory. Overall, I'm still fairly happy with my design. The story and photos were provided by my instructor, but everything else is "from scratch." The orange and green colors used throughout were taken from the photo on the first spread.

If I were doing this assignment today, I would probably do the photos a little differently so that there was more consistency throughout for both the photo sizes and the columns.

Magazine Resdesign

My final assignment was to redesign the cover, index page, a department spread, and feature page of a magazine. I'm still happy with the cover and index pages, but there are some things I would do differently on the department and feature pages.

On the department page, I would rework a few of the elements so that it all flowed together better. On the feature page, I would redesign the islands. Every element on that page is type, including the arrow going across the top of the page. Were I to design this today, the paths to the islands would be replaced with arrows similar to those across the top of the page, and would stick to the word cloud idea used for the states for each island.
