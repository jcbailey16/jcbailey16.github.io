Web Design

On this page, you will find samples of my website design work as a front-end engineer at Raving Software, as well as a handful of school projects completed while I was a student at AACC. I am including samples from both sources to show a range of my abilities.

Raving Software

Raving Software is a web design company I worked at from 2017 - 2018. Part of my responsibilities included planning, designing, and coding websites for our clients and for the company's website. The Raving Software website started as an HTML-based site, but we converted it to a WordPress website for easier management and updates, and to be maintain a consistent look between the blog and static informative content. All client websites made for Raving Software were created within the WordPress framework.

Marie Chantal's Bakery

For this small bakery, we developed a theme through establishing a color scheme, font choices, and repeated elements. In addition, we improved her online ordering and reviewing processes, and trained the client on how to update her photo galleries and the image slide, and how to manage reviews submitted via the website.

The Delightful Cupcake

This is a website I built on my own as a theme example for Raving Software. We wanted to showcase our abilities and give clients an idea of what we could do for their website. Having examples of other websites helps the client to figure out what they do, don't like, and what might work best for their business needs.

Silver Star Service Center, Inc.

We built the website to assist in the business's workflow by incorporating service appointment scheduling, a vehicle sales section, an on-site customer rating capability, and customer contact forms, all of which can be accessed by the client through the Wordpress CMS.

Casa Della Nonna

This is a single-page website for a local restaurant's menu. The site is mobilized, though I would go back and fix the H1 headline size as it takes up too much space vertically when the browser window is small. Other than that, I'm happy with how this website turned out. (AACC project)

Type and Positioning Web Page

For this web page, I had to choose a typeface and design a digital typeface poster than conveyed it's personality. I chose the Google Font of Alegreya and used shapes and text, per the assignment requirements, to create a page of an open book. I chose to create a book because the font was designed for works of literature. (AACC project)

Explore: Get Out and Go

As a final project, I designed a multi-page and responsive website. I chose to build a website that would focus on providing people with activities to do in the Baltimore-Annapolis-D.C. area. Certain elements, such as tables, lists, and contact forms, had to be utilized in the website. Besides the requirements, I had complete control over the creative choices. I used my own photos or created graphics for the website where I could, and used Creative Common licensed photos for the rest. The background is actually a repeated image of the blue color that I texturized in PhotoShop. There's a back-up background of the solid color blue in case the images can't load. (AACC project)

Friends with Paws

Another class also required a multi-page, responsive website for a final assignment. Instead of reusing my "Explore" website, I decided to do another one from scratch. While it does mobilize, it doesn't execute as well as I would like. The structure I used for the "Explore" website worked much better than what I did for this one. I have since had more real-world experience and taken a class that focuses on responsive design, and improved my responsive coding and planning. (AACC project)

Science and the Sea

The purpose of this assignment was to rebrand an existing podcast website and make it responsive. I started building the site at 320px and decided on media query breakpoints when the content started to look bad at a certain width. The new technique used here was image swapping using the picture element, source tag, and media queries to apply an appropriately sized and cropped image. I'm happy with how this website looks on small to medium-size screens, however there are some sizing issues on large screens that I needed to work on a bit more to keep content from getting too wide.(AACC project)

Novel! Publications

For this assignment, I had to come up with a product to sell and a fake company in order to implement a checkout workflow using online forms. In addition to using forms, this responsive site experimented with HDPI images and flex display. I created a small publishing company that focuses on the Young Adult genre. There are some positioning issues at larger screen sizes, but, overall, it looks and functions pretty well. Currently, the forms do not function (accept input, validate, etc.), as that was outside the scope of this assignment, but it is something I may go back and work on at a later time. (AACC project)

Typographics

This was a final project for my responsive web design class in which we combined all the techniques we had learned throughout the semester: image swapping, HDPI images, SVG's, forms, and flex display. The assignment was to create a conference website for Typographics. I was given the text content and most of the artwork, but was responsible for creating a brand and logo for the website. I'm really happy with how this website turned out. I structured and styled my pages in a way that a lot of my positioning and sizing issues from earlier assignments were not an issue this time around. What did I do differently? I started implementing max-widths on photos and paragraph elements. In addition, I used jQuery to create a drop-down menu and modals for the workshops, tours, and bookseller details. Everything does work, as long as JavaScript is enabled on the device. One of my goals is to learn alternatives to JavaScript so that if a device doesn't have it enabled, a function or effect can still execute. (AACC project)
