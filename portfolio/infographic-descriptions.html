Infographic Descriptions

Missouri Energy Providers

The graphs show the total electricity production capacity breakdown by fuel type in 2011 for three of Missouri's biggest energy providers: Empire District Electric Co., Kansas City Power & Light, and Ameren Missouri. Utilities do not always produce energy at full capacity. If each company was generating and purchasing electricity at full capacity in 2011, the following percentages of energy would come from each type of fuel. The maps show the areas each of the companies service in Missouri.

This infographic was a group effort between myself and two other students. Overall it was an easy collaboration as we mainly had to agree on and stick to the same color palate for the graphic's key. Each person worked on respresenting the requested data for one energy provider. I worked on Kansas City Power and Light's data. Though a relatively simple graphic, the locator map was challenging in that the services areas did not neatly stay within the counties. However, replicating the service area allowed me to practice with the Illustrator pen tool when I first learning how to use the software.

College Avenue Median

College Avenue is a major road in Columbia, Missouri, that borders on side of the university. Various types of student housing was located across the street from the campus. Often, students would cross at any point along the way to get home and stand in the road as they waited for it to be clear to continue crossing. The City Council authorized funds to go towards making the road safer for everyone. In addition to adding two crosswalks to the stretch of road that borders the main campus, they project included a median to prevent pedestrians from crossing anywhere along the road.

This was one of many locator maps I made as an infographic designer for the Missourian. The print version, seen here, was straightforward to make. The online version provided more of a challenges as we (myself and the TA) tried to make it interactive so the explanations would show when each number was hovered over. This was ultimately achieved by using Flash.

Notable Properties in Columbia, Missouri

This infographic shows some of the Historic Preservation Commission's 2013 list of Most Notable Places in Columbia, Missouri. The properties shown here are located in downtown Columbia. At the time, the Niedermeyer Apartments were up for sale and many in the city were fighting to have it saved. The building was the oldest building in downtown Columbia and many did not want it to be demolished.

This was one of two infographics I did for this story. This particular piece ran alongside a piece that discussed the history of the notable properties for 2013.

The Niedermeyer Building

This infographic ran with a follow-up story to the initial news the Niedermeyer Apartments were up for sale and in danger of being demolished. The graphic was meant to show readers where the building was located in downtown Columbia both with street names and popular nearby businesses readers would be familiar with.

The building was ultimately sold to Nakhle Asmar, an MU mathematics professor, who immediately set to work on fixing up the apartments. As of 2018, the apartments were still standing.

Sinkholes: Nature's Disappearing Act

Unlike the other showcased infographics, this one was not requested to run alongside a story. Instead, it is a final project that was compiled to run as a feature piece in the Missourian over the summer. This assignment required three components of which one had to be interactive.

The topic of my project is sinkholes, chosen for a number of occurrences that had taken place in Midwest that spring. My elements included the stages of sinkhole formation, a map of the U.S. that shows the states where sinkholes are common, and a sinkhole density map of Missouri with facts about sinkholes. In the online version, the stages we presented in a JavaScript-enabled slideshow, and the sinkhole facts showed when different areas of the sinkhole density map of Missouri were hovered on.
