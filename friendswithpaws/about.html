About Us

Friends with Paws started in 1996 in a small building just off Annapolis. Originally, we could only house 70 dogs and cats at a time. Today, our facility can hold up to 200 animals, including dogs, cats, rabbits, hamsters, gerbils and other small pets. We are a non-profit, no-kill shelter. All money from adoptions, veterinarian care, and fundraisers goes back to the shelter to take care of the animals and maintain the staff and facilities.

Check out our blog

Looking for pet tips or (pet)life hacks? Wondering if a pet's behavior is a sign of illness? We have the answers!

Our blog is updated once or twice a week by our staff members. From health information to training tips, we have you covered.

Don't see a topic or have a suggestion? Send us an email or fill our contact form. We look forward to hearing from you.

Our mission is to find a forever home for every animal in our facility and to educate the community about proper animal care. A lot of animals that come through our doors are from homes where their owners didn't realize everything that went into keeping a pet. To fight this, we host pet care forums and welcome the public to join us for in-house dog training sessions (you can bring your own dog or work with one of ours). We believe the more knowledge people have, the less overwhelming it will be when they bring home a new pet.

We have an amazing staff, from experienced veterinarians and vet techs to a dedicated management and front office staff. That said, volunteers are our life blood. We would not be half as success as we are without our volunteers. We know life can be chaotic so we accept volunteers all the time. We don't have time requirements and only ask for at least 24 hours notice if you're unable to make your shift. All of us at Friends with Paws look forward to seeing you, especially our furry residents.

We are opened Monday through Friday from 10am to 6pm, and Saturday from 10am to 2pm. We are closed on holidays.
