About the Book

In an effort to help her overburdened parents, 10-year-old Fanny Price is taken to Mansfield Park to live with her rich Uncle and Aunt Bertram and their four children: Thomas, Edmund, Maria, and Julia. Though she benefits from her uncle’s wealth, Fanny is never allowed to forget she is not equal to her cousins. With her older cousin Edmund as her only friend, Fanny’s grows up feeling lonely and dreaming of life with her eight siblings in Portsmouth.

The arrival of siblings Mary and Henry Crawford cause things to change when Fanny is 18. The Crawfords, who are visiting their own aunt and uncle from London, are bored with country life and are desperate for “entertainment.” Fanny’s cousins are immediately drawn to their glamorous and romantic ways, thus becoming the Crawfords’ unwitting prey. The Crawford siblings also force Fanny to struggle between her sense of familial duty and her duty to herself.
