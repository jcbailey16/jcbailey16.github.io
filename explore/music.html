Annapolis

Spring Art Show

From April 2 to May 31, the McBride Gallery is hosting its spring show, "Breaking the Rules."

According to gallery owner Cynthia McBride, the show was inspired by artists who "break the rules" but still create a good painting. This event is meant to explore how that can happen by hosting a reception for the artists on April 2.

Admission is free, and the artwork is available for purchase.

Baltimore

Come see 'Something Rotten"

We have 10 tickets for the April 23 performance of "Something Rotten" at the Hippodrome Theater in Baltimore.

From New York Magazine: "Set in 1595, this hilarious smash tells the story of Nick and Nigel Bottom, two brothers who are desperate to write a hit play. When a local soothsayer foretells that the future of theatre involves singing, dancing and acting at the same time, Nick and Nigel set out to write the world’s very first MUSICAL! With its heart on its ruffled sleeve and sequins in its soul, Something Rotten! is “The Producers = The Book of Mormon x The Drowsy Chaperone. Squared!"

Sign up by April 16 for tickets, using the activity code "Something Rotten."

STEM Exhibit

Port Discovery Children's Museum is hosting "STEM in Spring: Spring Break Week" from April 8 to April 17.

Hours
  • M-F: 9:30am to 5pm
  • Sat.: 10:30am to 5pm
  • Sun.: Noon to 5pm

From the museum: "Spring break is here, but children can still learn and play at Port Discovery during our Spring Break STEM in Spring activities. Programs and activities will center around Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math, with new activities and programs every day of spring break. Enjoy activities like Eric Energy’s “Spark Attack” show, interactive plays, Wildlife Adventures, pop-up “E-lite-tricity” activities, making groovy lava lamps, and more. We’ll also have special workshops including “Journey Inside a Cell” where children will enter a life-sized cell to learn about the components of a cell and their important roles, a Virobot workshop and more!"

Washington, D.C.

Tram Tour at the National Arboretum

Try something new for mother's day this year: The U.S. National Arboretum is having an open-air tram tour during the two-week bloom for their azaleas.

Dates
  • April 25
  • April 26
  • May 2
  • May 14

You must register for this event and there is a $15 per person fee.

About this event: "Stroll along wooded hillside trails amid a brilliant spring display of blooming azaleas while learning about the origins and culture of these popular shrubs. Barbara Bullock, long-time curator of the renowned Arboretum Azalea Collections, tells the story of how Arboretum research has contributed hundreds of new varieties for gardeners, gives expert advice on all aspects of growing azaleas, and answers your questions.
